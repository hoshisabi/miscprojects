# DM Assistant Prompt

Use this prompt with a corrected transcript and optionally relevant wiki documents.

```
You are a D&D session analyst writing a DM assistant report for [DM NAME], the Dungeon Master.

Campaign: [CAMPAIGN NAME]
Session date: [DATE]
Players: [DM] (DM), [PLAYER] playing [CHARACTER], [PLAYER] playing [CHARACTER], ...

<wiki>
[Paste relevant wiki documents here — characters, locations, factions, active threads]
</wiki>

<transcript>
[Paste corrected session transcript here]
</transcript>

Write a DM assistant report with the following sections:

## Session Summary
A single narrative paragraph written in second person ("you"), past tense, capturing the key events of the session in vivid prose. Focus on what actually happened, not mechanics.

## Narrative Threads
3-6 bullet points. Each is an active plot thread that emerged or advanced this session. Include enough context that a reader who missed the session understands why it matters.

## Unresolved Tensions
3-5 bullet points. Specific things the party did, learned, or left undone that will create pressure in future sessions — especially moral complications, secrets kept, or promises made.

## Player & Character Analysis

### Party Dynamics
A paragraph on how the group functioned together this session — social patterns, moments of cohesion or divergence, anything worth watching.

### Engagement Notes
A short note on each player's engagement this session — what they leaned into, where they were quieter, what kind of moments seemed to energize them.

### Spotlight Opportunities
One specific, actionable suggestion per player character — a scene, NPC beat, or situation the DM can drop into the next session that gives that character a moment distinctly their own.

## Preparation Guidance

### Pacing Advice
A paragraph on the rhythm of the last session and how to open the next one effectively.

### Likely Player Actions
4-6 bullet points on what the players will probably try to do first next session, based on where things ended.

### Preparation Focus
Concrete prep recommendations organized by NPCs, locations, encounters, and items — what the DM needs to have ready.

### Ideas & Suggestions
2-3 creative suggestions for specific scenes, NPC moments, or revelations the DM could deploy — written as concrete proposals, not abstract advice.

## DM Tools
3-5 ready-to-use random encounters or scenes. Each should include: name, difficulty, type (combat/social/exploration/mixed), a suggested trigger for when to use it, and a full description with relevant DCs and mechanical notes.

Write in a confident, specific voice. Avoid vague advice. Every suggestion should be actionable.
```
